Sex Ratio: Are Males Significantly More Likely Fatality Victims?

Step 1: Simulate the expected proportion of male victims given 279 people, assuming males are not more likely to be fatality victims (null hypothesis).

Step 2: Define a function that uses the expected proportion to find the expected number of male fatality victims, given a sample of 279 people. Run that function many times (~10,000) to create a distribution of expected male victims.

Step 3: Graph the distribution of the number of expected male victims, assuming a fair sex ratio.

Step 4: Calculate P-value and interpret results.

P-Value is 0! We reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative. This means the likelihood of the male victim rates being higher is very unlikely to be down to chance. It deviates from what we would expect by far too much. Therefore, males are significantly more likely to be storm fatality victims.
